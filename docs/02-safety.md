# 2. Safety

**This document is mandatory reading before operating the machine.** It covers the four hazard classes present during operation and the rules of operation in force in this space.

## 2.1 Thermal hazards

The barrel, band heaters, and nozzle operate at **180–260 °C**, well above the threshold for immediate, serious contact burns.

- Treat the entire barrel assembly as hot at all times. It remains at burn-hazard temperatures for **30 minutes or more after shutdown**, depending on ambient conditions.
- Wear heat-resistant gloves for any work near the barrel, including cleaning.
- Extruded filament is molten at the nozzle. Handle it only with pliers or tweezers until it has passed the cooling zone.
- Keep flammable materials (paper, solvents, loose filament) clear of the heated zone.

## 2.2 Fume hazards

Melting thermoplastics releases fumes. PLA produces the mildest emissions; **ABS releases styrene and must not be processed in an unventilated space.**

Current required practice:

- Operate the machine with active ventilation (open window with extraction fan, or a fume hood where available).
- Do not melt material in an occupied, unventilated room.
- A strong burnt-plastic odor indicates excessive temperature or material stagnating in the barrel. Stop and investigate.

## 2.3 Mechanical hazards

- The auger and the puller rollers exert sufficient force to cause injury. Keep fingers, tools, and loose clothing away from the hopper throat and the puller nip while motors are energized.
- Tie back long hair; remove lanyards, loose sleeves, and jewelry before operation.
- Feed filament into the puller with tools, never by hand.

## 2.4 Electrical hazards

- The machine is mains-powered via a power supply and heater circuit. Do not operate with wet hands or near liquids.
- **Verify the power supply voltage selector matches the local mains supply (110 V / 220 V) before first power-on.** An incorrect selector position is a known first-run fault.
- If wiring is loose, discolored, or emits a hot odor: power off at the wall, remove from service, and tag the machine accordingly.

## 2.5 Rules of operation

1. **The machine is never left running unattended.** No exceptions.
2. First-time operators must complete their first production run under the supervision of an experienced operator.
3. Every session is logged (operator, material, temperature, outcome) per the [Experiment Log](07-experiment-log.md).
4. In case of uncertainty at any point: power off and consult an experienced operator or the maintainer.

## 2.6 Emergency reference

| Situation | Action |
|-----------|--------|
| Burn | Cool the affected area under running water for 20 minutes. Seek medical attention for anything beyond a minor first-degree burn. |
| Smoke from the barrel | Cut power at the wall. Do not open or purge. Ventilate the area and allow the machine to cool fully before investigating. |
| Entanglement in puller or auger | Cut power at the wall **before** attempting to free the obstruction. |
| Electrical odor or sparking | Cut power at the wall, unplug, tag the machine out of service, and report to the maintainer. |
